A European Tier 1 automotive supplier was operating a fragmented distribution network inherited from multiple acquisitions. With 12 DCs across 4 countries and inconsistent carrier contracts, logistics costs had risen to 11.4% of revenue — well above the 7–8% industry benchmark.

The challenge

Legacy DC footprint from 3 acquisitions, 47 carrier relationships, no network optimisation model, and logistics cost growth outpacing revenue at 3.2% per year.

Our approach

Months 1–3: Diagnostic & baseline
Full network cost model built; carrier benchmarking completed across all lanes
Months 4–8: Network redesign
Modelled 14 network configurations; selected 7-DC hub-and-spoke design
Months 9–18: Implementation
Phased DC consolidation, new carrier contracts, and WMS standardisation

The outcome

12 DCs consolidated to 7 regional hubs. Carrier relationships reduced from 47 to 12 strategic partners with volume-leveraged rates. Logistics cost fell from 11.4% to 7.9% of revenue, saving $18M annually. Average delivery lead time reduced by 2 days across the customer base.

A global CPG business with 6,000 SKUs across 28 markets was running a 12-week demand planning cycle based on sales history and sales team input. Forecast accuracy averaged 62% at SKU level, resulting in $120M+ in excess inventory and chronic stockouts on fast-moving lines.

The challenge

62% average SKU-level forecast accuracy, $120M excess stock, and a planning team spending 70% of their time on data preparation rather than decision-making.

Our approach

Months 1–2: Data architecture
Unified 8 data sources into a single demand signal layer including POS, distributor sell-out, and weather
Months 3–6: Model development
Deployed ensemble ML model with separate algorithms per SKU cluster; planner override UI built
Months 7–12: Embedding & capability
Full S&OP process redesign; planning team upskilled on model interpretation and exception management

The outcome

SKU-level forecast accuracy reached 99.1% on the top 80% of volume. Safety stock reduced by 34%, releasing $45M in working capital. Planning team now spends 80% of their time on value-added decisions rather than data prep.

A European pharmaceutical manufacturer with 140 active API and packaging suppliers had zero real-time visibility beyond Tier 1. Critical line shutdowns were occurring 3–4 times per year due to supply disruptions that could have been predicted weeks in advance.

The challenge

3–4 critical line shutdowns per year at $2.8M average cost each. 26-day average order-to-delivery on critical materials. No Tier 2/3 supplier visibility.

Our approach

Months 1–6: Architecture & supplier onboarding
Control tower platform selected; API integrations built for top 40 suppliers by criticality
Months 7–14: Full rollout
All 140 suppliers onboarded; ML risk scoring deployed with 6-week forward horizon
Months 15–24: Optimisation
Lead time reduction program run in parallel; 22 process improvements implemented across procurement

The outcome

Order-to-delivery lead time reduced from 26 days to 11 days on critical lines. Zero line shutdowns in the 18 months following full deployment. The control tower now surfaces risk signals an average of 19 days before impact, enabling proactive intervention.

A heavy equipment manufacturer was running at 54% OEE — 26 points below best-in-class. Unplanned downtime was the single biggest constraint on capacity, costing an estimated €22M per year in lost production and expediting costs.

The challenge

54% OEE. Top 3 downtime causes responsible for 68% of total lost time. No structured maintenance program. Shop floor KPIs not cascaded below plant manager level.

Our approach

Months 1–4: Loss analysis & prioritisation
Granular OEE measurement deployed; Pareto of losses built; quick-win roadmap defined
Months 5–15: Lean implementation
5S, SMED, TPM programs run in parallel; daily management system embedded across all lines

The outcome

OEE improved from 54% to 73% — a 19-point gain. Unit manufacturing cost reduced by 28%. Throughput increased 23% without capital investment. The improvements generated €19M in annualised value, returning 8x the engagement fee in year one.
